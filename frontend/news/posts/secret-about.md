## How <span style="color:#f5c542;">EFTForge</span> Started

The project started off as a [db4tarkov](https://db4tarkov.com) clone, it was even called `db4tarkov CN` before the project was rebranded to <span style="color:#f5c542;">EFTForge</span>. My site is designed specifically for the Chinese playerbase, with UI/UX and site performance pinned as the **TOP priority**. As some of you might know, there are a lot of questionable UI design choices on [db4tarkov](https://db4tarkov.com), and the localization for the site barely works. So I changed that.

The early versions of the site was `dogshit="true"`.

![EFTForge v0.0.7, 2026-02-20](./news/images/ee/EFTForgev0.0.7.jpg "EFTForge v0.0.7, 2026-02-20")

I had hardcoded gun data and EvoErgo formulae that I pulled straight out of my ass.

But the API fetch for the guns worked. Kind of.

Over time, the API integration got real, the build calculator got smarter, and the EvoErgo formula rewritten. The UI/UX went from "functional" to something I'm actually proud of. I build every element of this site from the ground up, with the help of AI _(Yes I vibe code, go and take a walk)_. And then with patch v0.7.0 came Chinese localization. I was finally confident enough to record a full site functionality demonstration to post onto bilibili:

## The Toy Gun

The **20x1mm Toy Pistol** is a weapon in Escape from Tarkov. It fires plastic discs. It does 1 (one) damage. And It has no meaningful modding tree.

It also has absolutely no business being in a weapon modding tool.

And yet here it is. **Pinned to the top. Always.** _(intentional behavior, not a bug)_

I kept it because it gives my site identity and soul. Not too business-sy and overly formal.

My in-game profile also has 3 toy guns pinned! Feel free to add me on EFT, search for `M0rph1ne`!
